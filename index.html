Project Idea:

Overview:

The project I have thought of is a modification of the existing doorbell but with an addition of a light and an led screen which can provide a message indicating that someone has rung the doorbell. This would work the same way as the normal doorbell however having the added features of an led screen and a light that could flash or be solid depending on how often the doorbell was rung within a given period. This has the benefit of being seen by the person in the house without necessarily having to be heard if the person is wearing headphones or in another room.

Motivation:

Although the popularity of doorbells in the younger generations has decreased. There is more utility to a visual representation or message indicating someone is at the door. This product has the benefit of aiding those with hearing loss. 1 in 6 Australians suffers from hearing loss which means 4.1 million Australians in the country have some level of hearing loss. As younger people are more frequently listening to music at higher levels the percentage of people with hearing loss is expected to be 1 in 4 by 2050. Therefore when people cannot hear their doorbell ring having a visual representation is very necessary.

Description:

The features of this project will be the doorbell wiring to a speaker/led lights and mini led screens, which will emit a loud sound when the doorbell is rung. Another feature is a mini light like led beads which will emit light when the doorbell is rung. This could be added to each room in the house to alert the owner wherever they are in the house. A flashing function could be added when the doorbell is pressed multiple times within a given period. There will also be a mini led screen that could to placed on the wall alongside the led lights in each room. The message on the led screen could vary along with the led light flashing indicating the doorbell ringer is pressing it multiple times within a given period. An example: you are studying in your bedroom at night and the doorbell rings. You are wearing headphones and do not hear them. However, since there is an led light and led screen you see someone has rung the doorbell. Depending on the time you may not want to answer the doorbell. However, the led light could start flashing repeatedly indicating the person is pressing the doorbell frequently and the message on the led screen could state something along the lines of “The doorbell ringer is frantic” or “They could be dangerous” or “An emergency could be occurring”. A microcontroller such as an: Adruino or Raspberry pi would have to be used to control the input and outputs of the doorbell being rung. If a Raspberry pi was used then the programming could be completed through the python language and utilise idle and the editor. If the Arduino were used then the Arduino IDE program would have to be installed to program the Arduino. The program could be made available for the admin ( the owner of the doorbell) to control the flashing frequency of the led light and the message that appears on the led screen. Another byproduct of using my specific doorbell would be an ability to disable the audio sound, meaning the speaker. This could be immensely useful to people who have young children or babies. Since babies are notoriously hard to get to sleep the annoyance of someone ringing the doorbell could be very bothersome to the parents. With the led screen and light the parents could still be alerted of the person ringing the doorbell without having to wake up the baby could be very useful for couples with children. Another feature along with the ability to disable audio would be the freedom to set up the led light and led screens wherever necessary. For example: in the bedroom, you may not want an led light to flash at night when you are sleeping as it may interrupt your sleep patterns. Instead, a dimmer led screen that won’t necessarily wake you up or interrupt your sleep. Whereas in your study room you may want a bright led screen since you listen to music while studying and will not hear the person right the doorbell.
